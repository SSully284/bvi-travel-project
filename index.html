Why the British Virgin Islands?

Explore warm sands and beautiful reefs

This British Overseas Territory consists of 4 larger islands: Tortola, Anegada, Virgin Gorda, and Jost Van Dyke. There are also 32 smaller islands and islets that make up the British Virgin islands, of which more than 20 are uninhabited. There are 63 popular dive sites in the BVI, which include 57 coral reef sites and 6 artificial reefs that have been created by shipwrecks.

Sailing is one of the favourite sports in the British Virgin Islands. The activity is a popular attraction for tourists who have fun in anything from small boats to fully decked-out and crewed luxury yachts can be rented or chartered. Other water-based activities include island-hopping, poker-run, snorkeling, windsurfing, scuba diving, fishing, and more. With clean pristine beaches and some of the finest sand (Loblolly beach, Anegada) you are sure to make memories you won't forget.
